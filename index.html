> The Major Qualifying Project is the senior capstone research project at WPI.

> My project was entitled "Deep Learning for Mental Health Screening Using Smartphone Data", and took place throughout both semesters of my senior year. The goal of this project was to test the accuracy of many different learning models' ability to predict depression diagnoses based on a patient's text message content. On this project team, I was primarily responsible for implementing natural language processing and sentiment analysis technigues to analyze the messages, and streamlining and fixing the data pipeline for the project.

The Interactive Qualifying Project is a social- science research project at WPI completed during junior year which connects STEM subjects to societal issues. The project team I worked with was tasked with creating a "passive data collection" plan to synthesize customer feedback for the United States Patent and Trademarks Office. This plan outlined ways the USPTO could collect customer satisfaction data passively and methods that could be used to interpret the data collected. We worked with the office of Patent Quality Assurance to determine what ethical and legal practices should be followed, and presented them with an outline of a data collection plan, a literature review of prior research and best practices and next steps for moving forward.
